Datasheet parity is the starting point, not the proof. A 1:1 replacement is confirmed on your own line: run the Censil grade at the same dosage as your current import, compare gloss at 60° and 85°, clarity on a dark panel, touch and can stability — then fine-tune dosage within ±0.3%. Typical evaluation cycle is 1–2 weeks, and we ship a free 2 kg sample with a 1:1 benchmark sheet for exactly this.

Frequently asked questions

Why is there no side-by-side table on this page?

Because we could not find a Grace-published datasheet for a grade designated SYLOID® ED 4. Grace's EMEA product range lists ED 2, ED 3, ED 5, ED 30, ED 40, ED 44, ED 50, ED 52, ED 56 and ED 80. Rather than print reference figures we cannot source, we state the Censil specification and ask you for the exact grade name.

I do use a grade called ED 4. What should I send you?

The grade name exactly as printed on your drum, COA or purchase order, plus your resin system and target gloss. It may be a regional designation or a grade outside the EMEA range. We will map the 1:1 equivalent and issue the benchmark sheet against the correct reference.

What is Censil361C for?

Universal semi-matte and matte finishes in waterborne wood coatings, industrial wood coatings and general matte plastic-coating development. It is the lead grade for our Universal Stable solution — usually the first stage of an import-substitution programme.

Yes. Tell us your resin system, target gloss and the grade you use today. A free 2 kg sample ships with the relevant TDS and a REACH Declaration of Conformity.

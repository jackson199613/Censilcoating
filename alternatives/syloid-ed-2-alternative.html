Quick answer

Which Censil grade replaces SYLOID® ED 2, and how close is it?

Censil651 runs at d50 4.9 – 5.2 μm against Grace's published 4.5 μm for SYLOID® ED 2 — slightly coarser, in the same fine-touch band. The bigger difference is deliberate: Grace lists ED 2 as untreated, while Censil651 is wax-modified. That wax layer is what controls anti-settling and surface touch in a waterborne system, where an untreated silica is more prone to hard sediment.

Frequently asked questions

SYLOID® ED 2 is untreated and Censil651 is wax-modified. Is that still a 1:1 replacement?

The target is the finish, not the datasheet. The wax modification is what makes the grade behave in a waterborne system — it is the parameter behind anti-settling and touch. Run Censil651 at your current ED 2 dosage and compare gloss, clarity and touch on your own line.

Will the wax modification change my dispersion process?

No special equipment is required. Add the matting agent after resin letdown under moderate shear, as you would with any fine-particle silica.

What is the pore volume of SYLOID® ED 2?

Grace does not publish a numerical pore volume or BET surface area for ED 2 in its EMEA product properties table. Censil651 is published at 1.6 – 1.8 mL/g and 250 – 280 m²/g.

Does Censil651 cause hard sediment in storage?

The wax modification is specifically what addresses this. Ordinary untreated silica can hard-cake at the bottom of the can; verify on your own formulation with the free 2 kg sample before you commit.

Quick answer

Which Censil grade replaces SYLOID® ED 30, and how close is it?

Censil660 (d50 5.0 – 5.3 μm) is the closest particle-size match to SYLOID® ED 30 and the one to start with for water-white clear topcoats. Censil1650A (d50 6.2 – 6.5 μm) runs coarser and is the choice when you want a lower-gloss, deep-matte surface. Grace publishes ED 30 at 5.5 μm with organic surface treatment; we publish pore volume and surface area, which Grace does not.

Frequently asked questions

Is Censil660 a drop-in replacement for SYLOID® ED 30?

It is built as one: start at the same dosage you run today, then fine-tune within ±0.3% to land your target gloss. Datasheet parity is the starting point — the replacement is confirmed on your own line. We ship a free 2 kg sample with a 1:1 benchmark sheet for exactly this.

Should I choose Censil660 or Censil1650A?

Censil660 (d50 5.0 – 5.3 μm) for water-white clear topcoats where transparency and wood-grain depth matter. Censil1650A (d50 6.2 – 6.5 μm) for low-gloss deep-matte surfaces. If you're replacing ED 30 in a clear topcoat, start with Censil660.

Grace lists SYLOID® ED 30 for solvent-based systems. Does that matter?

Grace positions the ED series for solvent-based wood coatings and recommends its AQ, W and 7000 series for waterborne. Many Southeast Asian formulators nevertheless run ED 30 in waterborne systems. Censil grades are engineered for waterborne from the start — that is the point of the range.

What is the pore volume and surface area of SYLOID® ED 30?

Grace does not publish numerical pore volume or BET surface area for ED 30 in its EMEA product properties table — only a graphical matting-efficiency chart. Censil660 and Censil1650A are published at 1.6 – 1.8 mL/g pore volume and 250 – 280 m²/g surface area.

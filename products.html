Quick answer

Which Censil grade replaces the imported grade I use today?

Each Censil grade is built as a 1:1 drop-in for a specific European benchmark: Censil660 and Censil1650A for SYLOID® ED 30, Censil651 for ED 2, Censil351C for ED 3, Censil361C for ED 4, and Censil3851 for ACEMATT® TS 100 — while Censil1531D is a cost-optimized grade with no direct benchmark. All grades share 1.6–1.8 mL/g pore volume and 250–280 m²/g surface area, with d50 ranging from 4.8 to 9.0 μm. Everything ships in 20 kg multi-wall bags, palletized for containers, with a REACH Declaration of Conformity.

The technology

What makes a matting agent perform in waterborne systems

Performance in high-humidity waterborne coatings comes from surface chemistry and particle engineering — not just raw silica.

High-melt PE wax modification

Surface wax treatment prevents hard sedimentation in low-viscosity systems and delivers a fine, smooth touch.

Narrow particle-size control

Tight d50 control (3.5–9.0μm) balances transparency and matting efficiency for water-white clarity.

Matting + anti-settling combo

Paired with fumed-silica thixotropy for anti-settling stability across the waterborne window.

High-purity amorphous silica

Amorphous, high-purity dry basis — no crystalline silica — for zero haze on dark substrates.

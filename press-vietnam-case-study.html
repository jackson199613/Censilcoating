Vietnamese Wood Coating Manufacturer Cuts Matting Agent Costs 28% with Censil361C

A mid-size Southeast Asian waterborne wood-coating plant moved its semi-matte line from imported ACEMATT TS 100 to Censil361C — same performance, shorter lead time, significant cost savings.

Customer profile

A privately owned waterborne wood coating manufacturer based in Binh Duong, Vietnam, producing 120–180 tonnes of paint per month across three product lines: clear topcoats, pigmented primers, and coloured topcoats for furniture export. The plant serves both domestic furniture manufacturers and export-oriented coating distributors. Matting agent consumption: 3–5 tonnes per month, primarily in the clear topcoat line.

Customer name withheld at the customer's request. All performance data verified by the customer's QC laboratory and shared with permission.

The challenge

The plant had used Evonik ACEMATT TS 100 as its primary matting agent for over five years. While performance was consistent, three issues were eroding the plant's competitiveness:

  • Cost pressure: TS 100 pricing had risen 18% over two years, driven by EUR/VND exchange rate volatility and European energy surcharges. The matting agent had become the single most expensive raw material in the clear topcoat formulation.
  • Lead time: Orders shipped from Europe via Singapore, with a typical door-to-door lead time of 6–8 weeks. The plant was forced to carry 8–10 weeks of safety stock, tying up working capital.
  • MOQ constraints: The European distributor's minimum order quantity was 1 pallet (40 bags, 800 kg). For the plant's smaller product lines, this meant over-ordering and storing material for months.

"We were spending too much time managing logistics for a raw material that should be a commodity by now," said the plant's Technical Director. "The performance was fine, but the supply chain was killing our margins."

The evaluation

In March 2026, the plant requested a free 2 kg evaluation sample of Censil361C, Censilcoat's universal stable matting grade positioned as a 1:1 replacement for ACEMATT TS 100. The sample arrived within 8 days, accompanied by a physicochemical benchmark sheet and TDS.

The plant's QC team ran a structured three-stage evaluation:

Stage 1: Physicochemical benchmark (Day 1–2)

Side-by-side comparison of Censil361C and TS 100 across particle size, oil absorption, bulk density, and pH. All parameters fell within the customer's acceptance window (±5% of reference values).

Stage 2: Lab drawdowns (Day 3–5)

The team prepared two identical clear topcoat formulations — one with TS 100, one with Censil361C — at the same dosage (4.5% by weight on total formula). Drawdowns were tested on black/white opacity charts and natural oak panels:

Stage 3: Production trial (Day 8–10)

The team ran a 500 kg production batch using Censil361C in place of TS 100, with no changes to the formulation, dispersion time, or application parameters. The batch was sprayed on production furniture panels and compared against the standard TS 100 batch:

  • Gloss: 28–30 (60°) across 15 panels — within the customer's 25–35 acceptance range
  • Clarity: No visual difference identified by the QC team in a blind panel comparison
  • Viscosity stability: <5% change after 14 days storage at 25°C
  • Spray application: No nozzle clogging, no spattering, no flow differences reported by operators

The results

Based on the successful trial, the plant switched its entire clear topcoat line to Censil361C in May 2026. After two months of full production:

"The switch was almost boring, which is exactly what we wanted," the Technical Director said. "We didn't change the formula, we didn't change the process, and our customers didn't notice. The only thing that changed was our cost sheet."

Why it worked

Three factors made the transition smooth:

  1. Particle-size match. Censil361C's d50 of 6.0–6.5 µm falls within the same window as TS 100, so the matting efficiency at equal dosage was effectively identical. No re-formulation was needed.
  2. Surface treatment compatibility. Both grades use an organic wax surface treatment, ensuring similar dispersion behaviour in waterborne acrylic systems. The plant's existing high-speed dispersion protocol (30 min at 3000 rpm) worked without modification.
  3. Documentation readiness. The 1:1 benchmark sheet and REACH Declaration of Conformity provided with the sample meant the plant's quality team could approve the substitution without additional regulatory review.

About Censil361C

Censil361C is Censilcoat's universal stable matting agent, designed for general-purpose waterborne wood and plastic coatings. It is positioned as a 1:1 replacement for Evonik ACEMATT TS 100 and comparable mid-range grades. Key parameters: d50 6.0–6.5 µm, pore volume 1.8 mL/g, organic surface treatment.
